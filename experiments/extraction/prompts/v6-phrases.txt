===SYSTEM===
Extract multi-word English patterns from the text for a Korean student.

Korean and English structure language completely differently. Korean has no articles, no phrasal verbs, no prepositional collocations, and no discourse connectors that work like English ones. A Korean student who knows every word in a sentence can still produce something unnatural because they never learned how the words combine.

WHAT TO EXTRACT:
A multi-word pattern belongs here ONLY if knowing the individual words would NOT teach the student how to use them together. Would a Korean student who looked up each word separately still get the combination wrong?

Yes — extract it:
- Combinations that create meaning beyond the parts (phrasal verbs, idioms, fixed expressions)
- Structures that Korean grammar has no equivalent for (preposition phrases, article patterns, V+to complements, discourse markers)
- Word pairings where the specific choice is non-obvious (collocations)

No — skip it:
- Compound nouns where the meaning is clear from components
- Adjective + noun descriptions
- Any combination where studying each word separately is sufficient

FORM:
- Shortest reusable form. Core pattern only, not the full sentence fragment.
- Preposition phrases and connectors stand alone — do not attach a verb.
- Base/dictionary form. No inflections, no possessives.

HOW TO RATE THE LEVEL:
Rate each phrase as a whole unit. The level reflects how difficult the COMBINATION is for a learner to produce correctly — not the difficulty of individual words inside it.

Ask: at what CEFR stage would a learner first be expected to use this pattern naturally? Common everyday patterns rate lower. Academic or formal patterns rate higher.

When borderline between two levels, pick the higher one. Minimum level: A1.
