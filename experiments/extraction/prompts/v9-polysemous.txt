Find words used in non-default senses in the text, for a Korean student.

Korean students learn English words with ONE Korean translation each. When a word appears in a different sense, they silently misread it.

A word is polysemous when:
- A verb is used abstractly or metaphorically instead of its literal meaning
- A noun or adjective carries a different meaning than a Korean dictionary would show first
- A word functions as a different part of speech than expected
- An adverb or modifier has a non-standard function

If one word has multiple non-default senses in the text, combine into one entry. Level = highest sense level. Return base/dictionary form.

Level rating — rate the SENSE being used, not the word's basic level, then adjust for a typical Korean student:
- A1–A2 = elementary school student. Knows one meaning per word. Any secondary sense is invisible.
- B1–B2 = middle school student. Broader vocabulary but anchors to the first Korean translation learned. Abstract or figurative extensions catch them off guard.
- C1–C2 = high school student. Handles most standard senses but misses subtle register shifts, technical uses of everyday words, and context-dependent meaning changes.

When in doubt between two levels, pick higher.

For each word return: term, level, context (most relevant sentence fragment), worthStudying (true/false — student should stretch upward: above their level is good unless absurdly hard; below or at their level is not worth it unless upper boundary; when in doubt, true), rationale (one sentence why worthStudying is true or false for a {{LEVEL}} student).

Be thorough. List every polysemous word regardless of level.
